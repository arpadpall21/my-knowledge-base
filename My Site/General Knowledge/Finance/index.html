Concept & Acronym Description
Stock Types - Groth Stock: Companies that are expected to grow fast
- Value Stock: Companies that are undervalued (have lower stock price than the company's worth)
- Divident Stock: Companies that focuses on paying dividents
- Cyclical Stock: Companies sensitive for economicaly changes (ex: leasure, luxury)
- Defensive Stock: Companies resilient agains economicaly changes (ex: food, healhtcare)
Fund Types - ETF (Exchange-Trade Fund): investment fund of collection of assets (stocks, bond, commodities) focusing on a specific sector (usually on an index like healthcare, tech, etc...)
- Hedge Fund: Aggressively managed private fund
- Mutual Fund: Professionally managed collective fund
- Index Fund: track a specfic index (Index -> average performance of a group of companies (ex: S&P500))
- REIT (Real Estate Investment Trust): Companies that own properties and generate income, they pay out incomes as form of dividents to shareholders

Beta - Volatility compared to the overal market
- score higher than 1 = higher volatility compared the overal marked / score lower than 1 = lower volatility compared the overal marked
Book Value - Net value if all the companie's assets are sold and all debts paid out
Common Stock VS Prefered Stock - Common Stock: gives the owner voting right and potentialy high dividens (depending how the company is doing) (higher earning potential because if the company is doing well receives the remaining dividents after Preferend Stocks)
- Prefered Stock: owner has no voting right and receives a fixed amounth of divident (safer because fixed dividents are payed before Common Stocks)
DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging) - Investing money regularly (monthly or quarterly) in the market
- The idea is more shares are bought when the market is low than when it is high, and we are likely to end up with a satisfactory overall price for all our holdings
Debt to Equity - Companie's the total debt divided by all shareholderls equity
Dilusion (share dilusion) - Companie issues additional shares that has 2 effects: - dilution shares: existing shares could worth less because the company has more shares for the same perentage of ownership
- raises capital: additional shares raises capital for the company which could raise share value
EPS - (Earnings Per Share) how much profit the company makes per share
Equity - Real value Assets - Liabilities = Equity
- ex: House worth 300$, but has a loan of 200$ (300$ - 200$ = 100$ -> equity))
Futures - Contract that lock both the buyer and seller to exchange assets on a specific future date
- Usually the buyer pays a upfront when the contract is created
IPO - (Initical Public Offering) 1st share offers when a company goes public
Liquidity - How fast an asset can be bought or sold
Market Capitalization - Total value of a company based on it's stock price
Options - Contract that gives the right to the buyer, to buy/sell but not the obligation, to buy/sell an asset (ex: stock) at a predetermined price (strike price) before a specific date
- The buyer pays a premium to the seller upfront
- So basically the buyer can decide to buy/sell the asset before the expiration date (but looses the premium in any case). The seller receives the premium but he's required to sel the asset if the buyer decides before expiration
PE (Price Earning Ratio) - Compares the company's stock price to its earning per share, used to determine if the companie is over/under valued
Volatility - How much the price moves
Volume - Number of shares traded in a given period
