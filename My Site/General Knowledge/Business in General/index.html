Porter's 5 forces
Subject Description
Competition - How intense is the competition in the industry?
Substitutes - Are there alternative products or services that can replace the existing ones?
New Entrants - How easy it is for new competitors to enter the market?
Buyers Power - How much power do your buyers have over you?
Suppliers Power - How much power do your suppliers have over you?

Competitive Advantages
Subject Description
Cost Leadership - Offering products or services at a lower cost that competitors
Focus - Targeting a specific market segment or niche
Innovation - Continuosly improving products, services, or processes to stay ahead of competitors
Customer Service - Providing exceptional customer service to build loyality and retain customers
Brand Repulation - Building a strong brand reputation to attract and retain customers
Operational Efficiency - Streamlining operations to reduce cost and improve profitability (Economy at scale)
Strategic Partnership - Forming alliances with other companies to leverage their strengths and resources
Technology - Investing in technology to improve products, services, or processes
Intellectual Property - Protecting unique products, services, or processes through patents, trademarks, or copyrights
Network Effects - Building a large user base that attracts more users and creates a competitiove advantage
Data and Analytics - Leveraging data to gain insights into customer behavior, market trends, and operational performance to maek informed business decisions
Agility and Adaptability - Being able to quickly respond to changes in the market, customer preference, or competitive landscape to maintain a competitive advantage
Sustainability - Implementing environmentally and socially responsive practices to appeal to customers and differentiate from competitors
Talent and Cluture - Building a strong organizational culture and attractint top talent to drive innovation, productivity, and customer satisfaction
